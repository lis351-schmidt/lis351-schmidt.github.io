Speed Records to date for High Speed Trains

Home Past Present First High Speed Train Future Records

There have been multiple attempts by countless manufactureres and countries for who can build the fastest train in history however this list only comprises of the most notable ones to date since the creation of the "high speed train" of trains with conventional wheels.

Here are the most notable record attempts (in order of top speed):

  1. SNCF TGV POS

    Top Speed: 574.8 km/h (357 mph)

    Country: France

    Year: 2007

  2. CRH380BL

    Top Speed: 487.3 km/h (303 mph)

    Country: China

    Year: 2010

  3. Class 955 Shinkansen

    Top Speed: 443.0 km/h (275 mph)

    Country: Japan

    Year: 1996

  4. Hyundai Rotem HEMU-430X

    Top Speed: 421.4 km/h (262 mph)

    Country: South Korea

    Year: 2013

  5. Siemens Velaro E/Ave S-103

    Top Speed: 403 km/h (251 mph)

    Country: Spain

    Year: 2006

  6. ICE 3/DB BR 406

    Top Speed: 368.4 km/h (229 mph)

    Country: Germany

    Year: 2001

  7. ETR 500 Frecciarosa

    Top Speed: 362 km/h (225 mph)

    Country: Italy

    Year: 2009

  8. Siemens EuroSprinter

    Top Speed: 357 km/h (222 mph)

    Country: Germany

    Year: 2006

  9. BR Class 373/Eurostar e300

    Top Speed: 334.7 km/h (208 mph)

    Country: United Kingdom

    Year: 2003

  10. Class 1000 Shinkansen/Type-O

    Top Speed: 286 km/h (178 mph)

    Country: Japan

    Year: 1972

Specifications and descriptions of the trains:
SNCF TGV POS
The record setting trainset was a tuned version of the TGV POS and was built up of 2 power cars plus 3 passenger rail cars. Different from its usual 8-10 rail cars. No conventional wheeled train has beaten this speed record.
CRH380BL
One of the few unmodded trains to break a record. It was during normal service using its 16 car trainset and is the current record holder for the top speed of an unmodified production vehicle.
Class 966 Shinkansen
This was a prototype train also called the 300X. The purpose of the 300X was to text features and technology that would be later incorporated with other newer shinkansen trains in the future.
Hyundai Rotem HEMU-430X
This was a 6 car trainset that achieved its record during a maximum speed test. It is now in service in South Korea delivered from 2020-2021 with its official name of the EMU-320.
Siemens Velaro E/Ave S-103
This was also an unmodified train that set the record in 2006. It held the record until 2010 when the CRH380BL took it. This was a typical trainset of 8 cars. This train shares similarities with Germany's ICE 3 train as its the 2nd member of Siemen's Velaro Family of trains (Siemens being the manufacturer).
ICE 3/DB BR 406
This was another one of the unmodified trains to set the speed record up until 2006 (taken by the Siemens Velaro E/Ave S-103). This specific type of InterCity Express (ICE) train, BR 406, was developed to operate international routes and achieved its record duing an approval run where it had to reach 10% higher than what its approved speed was.
ETR 500 "Frecciarossa"
The Italian name "Frecciarossa" means red arrow, mirroring its red color of current trains. The model that set the record in 2009 was a test model. It is important to note that this record was considered an "indoor" record as the speed achieved by this train was done in the Monte Bibele tunnel. This trainset was slightly shorter comprised of 2 power cars and 8 passenger cars compared to its current 11 passenger cars.
Siemens EuroSprinter
This is the only train on this list that wasn't part of a high speed trainset. The record was set by the EuroSprinter ES64U, the universal varient of the locomotive, operated by OBB, an Austrian rail operator. These trains have been exported all over the world to do a variety of jobs like haul passenger services and freight. On this day, the locomotive set the record while pulling one railcar behind it and holds the record for the maximum speed of an electric locomotive, unmodded.
BR Class 373/Eurostar e300
This was also an unmodded train as it was done during a routine service. The speed was recorded near Nashenden Valley on the High Speed 1 line. What makes it more impressive is this is also the longest train to set a record as the trainset is comprised of 2 power cars and 18 passenger cars. Its official name is the Eurostar e300 to follow the naming convention of its predecessor, the Eurostar e320. The BR Class 373 name is the naming convention of British trains. BR standing for British Class.
Class 1000 Shinkansen/Type-O
The Class 1000 Shinkansen was the very first true "High Speed Train". The prototype achieved its speed record on a test track that is now a part of the Tohoku Shinkansen line. This train was the prototype of the later built Type-O Shinkansen that entered service in 1964.
