Speed Records to date for High Speed Trains

Home Past Present First High Speed Train Future Records

There have been multiple attempts by countless manufactureres and countries for who can build the fastest train in history however this list only comprises of the most notable ones to date since the creation of the "high speed train" of trains with conventional wheels.

Here are the most notable record attempts (in order of top speed):

  1. Top Speed: 574.8 km/h (357 mph), Country: France, Year: 2007, SNCF TGV POS
  2. Top Speed: 487.3 km/h (303 mph), Country: China, Year: 2010, CRH380BL
  3. Top Speed: 443.0 km/h (275 mph), Country: Japan, Year: 1996, Class 955 Shinkansen
  4. Top Speed: 421.4 km/h (262 mph), Country: South Korea, Year: 2013, Hyundai Rotem HEMU-430X
  5. Top Speed: 403 km/h (251 mph), Country: Spain, Year: 2006, Siemens Velaro E/Ave S-103
  6. Top Speed: 368.4 km/h (229 mph), Country: Germany, Year: 2001, ICE 3/DB BR 406
  7. Top Speed: 362 km/h (225 mph), Country: Italy, Year: 2009, ETR 500 Frecciarosa
  8. Top Speed: 357 km/h (222 mph), Country: Germany, Year: 2006, Siemens EuroSprinter
  9. Top Speed: 334.7 km/h (208 mph), Country: United Kingdom, Year: 2003, BR Class 373/Eurostar e300
  10. Top Speed: 286 km/h (178 mph), Country: Japan, Year: 1972, Class 1000 Shinkansen
